About

I was born and raised near the coast in Barry, South Wales (Yes the Gavin and Stacey place), before heading up to Scotland for university.

As a kid I was always drawing or playing guitar. I even tried writing songs at one point (they weren't great). Then I got into magic (both The Gathering and tricks) and cardistry, but gaming was the thing that really stuck. It's what eventually pushed me to learn coding, just so I could try to recreate the games I loved.

I went to the University of St Andrews to study Computer Science. Things went well for a while — I made the Dean's List and even picked up an award for my work in CS2006 Advanced Programming Projects — but my mental health hit rock bottom. After 3 years I had a breakdown, resulting in a suicide attempt, and a stint in a psychiatric ward, and I ultimately decided to step away. It was tough, but it gave me the space I needed to start putting myself back together.

While I was still at uni, I got my first taste of professional work as a Web Developer Intern, building things with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Around the same time, I started freelancing. I established connections through a Community Interest Company I co-founded and now help run, which supports people struggling with mental health. Most of that work was building temporary mini-sites, tweaking and expanding on existing frameworks. Over time, I picked up React, TailwindCSS, Next.js and TypeScript, and got hooked on frontend development.

These days I'm back home in South Wales, juggling freelance work and running the CIC while leveling up my dev and software engineering skills. I'm also on the lookout for a normal part-time job while I figure out where I want to go next.

Get in touch with me if you think we could work together: jonathan@justasillygoose.com
